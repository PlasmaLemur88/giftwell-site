## 1. Gifter Design Page

The main canvas where the gifter chooses media (poster or gif), theme,
and product, writes their message, and sets when the recipient gets
notified.

**Decisions:**
- Switch to Partyful-style layout. Move the preview/card to the right
  side of the page. Nest the chosen product with the poster on the right
  so the whole gifting experience is visible without scrolling.
- Shrink the design screen ~15%.
- Add a Save Draft button.
- Add a primary CTA at the bottom right — wording like "Next" / "Choose
  recipients" / "Select your recipients" (final wording TBD).
- Move the Preview button down to that same bottom-right area. Current
  eyeball icon at the top is so unclear Greg couldn't find it.
- Preview now shows both the unwrap experience and the email the
  recipient will receive.
- Replace the "Logo" placeholder on the unwrap card with a slot for the
  gifter's name so the recipient knows who sent it.
- Add hover-highlight on selectable areas (Partyful does this; reads as
  high-end).
- Filter pills on the media picker — like Partyful.
- Shrink the media modal. Change its button text to "Upload your own
  file."
- Drop animated foreground "elements" for now. Keep themes (gradient
  backgrounds), posters, and gifs. Brandon to provide new gradient
  backgrounds.
- Send scheduling lives on this page — not in a slide-out at checkout,
  which is too late. Choice is Send now or Schedule; if Schedule →
  reveal date picker. Tooltip: "This is the date the email will be sent
  to the recipient." Rename "Target Shipping" → working name "Recipient
  Notification Date." Date stays in the product (don't strip it):
  gifters need it for birthdays and dated events, and merchants need it
  to triage support tickets like "why didn't they get it?" → because the
  gifter scheduled it two months out.

**Open questions:**

- How the multi-step flow actually progresses. Brandon doesn't love a

## 2. Honoring the Gifter (Email + Unwrap Page)

Both the recipient email and the unwrap page need to make clear this
gift came from a real person — not from the merchant.

**Decisions:**
- Unwrap page has to feel like a real reveal. Recipient sees it and
  thinks "oh sick, I got this gift from this person." Show the gifter's
  name prominently. Preserve mystery — recipient shouldn't know what the
  gift is until they hit unwrap.
- Body copy across both surfaces is gifter-centric, not merchant-centric.
  Pull `{gifter_name}` into subject and body. Drop "from Acme Corp" from
  body copy; keep the merchant logo only.
- Example: "Sarah wanted to send you something special." / "Greg sent
  you a gift from Fino. Unwrap it."
- The email-to-unwrap transition stays plain for now. Clicking the email
  lands on the current unwrap version — make it sexier later.

**Decisions:**

## 8. Merchant Admin — Email Center

Manage the emails Giftwell sends on the merchant's behalf.

**Decisions:**
- Giftwell provides all email templates, ready-made. Merchants don't
  design from scratch — counter to the Gifted / Yotpo / Klaviyo-template
  model.
- Emails go out as Shopify notifications from the merchant's existing
  store email — not from a Giftwell-owned domain. Reason: merchant's
  domain is already warm; a Giftwell cold domain would tank
  deliverability.
- Merchant customization: pick a color → generates a gradient background
  for emails.
- Template includes Giftwell logo × store logo, "Unwrap your gift" CTA,
  "Fast email powered by Giftwell" attribution.
- Logo auto-inherits from the store. Fallback "Add your logo" field in
  Onboarding / Settings.
- Email types in scope:
  - **To recipient**: gift notification, unclaimed reminder, order
    confirmation, shipped, delivered, review request.
  - **To gifter**: milestone updates, campaign live, campaign wrap-up,
    order confirmation.
- Merchant digest email ("12 of 50 gifts unwrapped"): merchant picks
  cadence — every 10, weekly digest, or first & final only.
- Body copy framing is gifter-centric, not merchant-centric (see §2 for
  examples and full reasoning).
- The gifter never sees the recipient's email — only the unwrap preview
  — which is why the Preview button on the Gifter Design Page now also
  shows the email (see §1).
- Masking the sender as "Mysterious [Store]" to preserve the reveal was
  discussed but tabled — unclear if feasible.
